# Private School Registration — Fastest Path to FTO

## Core Strategic Insights

**Licensing Is About Predictability, Not Speed**: States reward clear, complete, curriculum-aligned applications — speed follows clarity.

**Curriculum Drives Compliance**: Aligning instructional models to state frameworks removes the majority of common approval obstacles. Frame the "2 hour school day" as a "2 hour individualized education program augmented by ed tech apps, followed by 6 hours of life skills workshops and enrichments."

**Most Operators Wait in Line When They Could Walk Through Two Doors at Once**: In Minimal/Notification states (TX, FL, AZ, CA, NC — majority of Alpha School markets), there is no state education approval process. Operators waste 3-6 weeks preparing packages for states that don't require them, while under-preparing for the building CO — the actual bottleneck. Classify each state's archetype within 48 hours of lease signature. In Approval Required states (MA, WA, NY, MD), the reverse: operators underestimate approval timelines and discover too late that a school committee or state board controls their opening date.

**Negative Media About Public Schools Can Kill Your Application Before It's Filed**: Public information implying their school system is bad or administrators are dumb is highly damaging. Put administrators at ease quickly — emphasize support for teachers and education. In Approval Required states with discretionary authority, political goodwill is a prerequisite.

**The ESA/Voucher Trap: Optional Programs Create Mandatory Complexity**: TX (TEFA), AZ (ESAs), FL (FES) have minimal baseline requirements — but choice program participation adds accreditation, testing, financial reporting layers that take months. Decide early: open first under minimal requirements then pursue eligibility? Or delay to satisfy program requirements upfront? Wrong sequencing costs a full semester of enrollment revenue.

## State Registration Archetype Framework

### Archetype 1: MINIMAL — No Registration/Approval Required (0 weeks post-CO)

| State | Requirements | Statute |
|---|---|---|
| TX | No registration, licensing, or TEA approval. Offer reading, spelling, grammar, math, good citizenship. | TEC §25.086 |
| AZ | No accreditation, registration, licensing, or approval. File affidavit with county superintendent ages 6-16. | ARS §15-802 |
| AR | No accreditation, registration, licensing, or approval from state. | US DOE (AR chapter) |
| AL | Non-church: register annually by Oct 10 with AL DOE. Church schools: exempt. No approval/licensing. | AL Code §16-28-1, §16-28-7 |
| UT | No state approval required. | EdChoice Rankings 2025 |

ESA/Voucher caveat: TX (TEFA), AZ (ESAs) add accreditation/reporting IF participating in choice programs. Optional — does not affect right to operate.

### Archetype 2: NOTIFICATION — Register/File, No Approval (0-2 weeks, concurrent with CO)

| State | Requirements | Statute |
|---|---|---|
| FL | Register with DOE + annual survey + owner fingerprinting. DOE has no jurisdiction over private schools. | FL Stat. §1002.42 |
| CA | File Private School Affidavit annually with CDE. Online, immediate confirmation. Not approval. | CA EC §33190 |
| NC | Notice of Intent to DNPE 30-60 days before operation. Fire/sanitation inspections. Annual standardized testing. | NC GS §115C-547–562 |
| DE | Register with DDOE (online). Annual enrollment/attendance reporting. DDOE does not approve/monitor curriculum. | 14 Del. C. §2703, §2704 |

### Tennessee — Category System (4-12 weeks)

Five-category system (SBE Rule 0520-07-02). Must be approved under one category. Alpha School targets: Category II (accrediting agency) or V (baccalaureate-degreed teachers). State Fire Marshal inspection required before approval. Category II can run concurrent with building work. (TCA §49-6-3001)

### Archetype 3: APPROVAL REQUIRED (4-16+ weeks)

| State | Approver | Key Gate | Statute |
|---|---|---|---|
| MA | Local school committee | "Thoroughness and efficiency" equal to public schools. No state appeal. | MA c.76 §1 |
| WA | State Board of Ed | Annual approval. Certificated teachers. 180d/1000hr. | RCW 28A.195.010 |
| NY | Board of Regents | Provisional charter (nonprofit) or commissioner consent (for-profit). Site visit. | NY Ed. Law |
| MD | MD State Board | Certificate of approval. Church orgs may be exempt. | MD Ed. §2-206(e) |

MA is uniquely risky — each town's school committee operates independently.

### Archetype 4: HEAVILY REGULATED (8-24+ weeks)

| State | Key Burden | Source |
|---|---|---|
| NV | Traffic studies + architectural renderings even for <20 students | EdChoice 2025 |
| ND | State approval + teacher certification | ND Century Code Title 15.1 |
| MI | Non-church schools: $50K cash reserves before opening | MI Revised School Code |
| IN | Teacher certification requirements | EdChoice 2025 |

### Archetype 5: WINDOWED / CALENDAR-DEPENDENT (0-6+ months added delay)

| State | Constraint |
|---|---|
| NY | Board of Regents: March 1 deadline → June approval |
| WA | Annual SBE approval cycle |
| MD | Fixed approval cycles; may block mid-year openings |

### Puerto Rico — LICENSING REQUIRED (8-16+ weeks, adaptive)

Compulsory licensing for all private K-12 (3 L.P.R.A. §148l). Teacher certification from PR Secretary of Education, facilities inspection, education plan, economic viability study. Authority: Board of Postsecondary Institutions under PR Dept of State (Law 212-2018).

## Insights (Action + QC)

**Classify Archetype First**: Within 48 hours of lease, classify state archetype with statutory citation. Wrong archetype = wrong plan.

**Map Regulatory Requirements**: Identify department, approval type, review cycle within 5 days. Prevents 3-5 week lag.

**Curriculum Alignment by Archetype**: Minimal/Notification: confirm no review exists, document exemption. Approval Required: complete curriculum crosswalk before submission.

**Documentation Completeness**: Approval Required states: zero missing elements in pre-submission QA. Notification states: prepare only what's required (often just an online form).

**Local Expertise in Approval States**: Engage education attorney before regulator contact in MA/WA/NY/MD. Saves 2-3 months. Unnecessary in Minimal/Notification states.

**Weekly Communication Cadence**: Approval Required/Windowed states only. Log outbound contact weekly; no gap >7 days. Reduces queue time 30-50%.

## Fast-Track Registration Blueprint

**All States (Day 1-2):** Classify archetype → Confirm ESA/voucher intent → Identify responsible department

**Minimal (TX, AZ, AR, UT, AL):** Confirm curriculum requirements → Focus on CO → ESA/voucher as parallel workstream if needed → Open when CO issued

**Notification (FL, CA, NC, DE):** File registration/NOI → Fire/sanitation inspections → Complete before/concurrent with CO (≤2 weeks)

**Tennessee:** Target Category II or V → Initiate SFMO inspection (Private.Schools@tn.gov) → Accrediting agency application concurrent with building (4-12 weeks)

**Approval Required (MA, WA, NY, MD):** Engage attorney (Wk 1) → Identify body + calendar (Wk 1) → Legal pre-review (Wk 2) → Submit package (Wk 3-4) → Weekly cadence (Wk 4+) → Plan for 12-16 weeks worst case

**Windowed (NY, WA, MD):** Identify next window BEFORE committing to lease → Evaluate early filing/provisional status if >3 months away

**QC:** Archetype classified with citation; filings submitted or confirmed unnecessary; timeline documented (best/worst case); ESA/voucher decision logged; no open items without next steps.

## Key Reference Facts

- No federal licensing; CO universally required regardless of archetype
- 34 states require registration; 25 require approval; 35 make accreditation optional (EdChoice 2025)
- Least restrictive: DE, UT, AR, AZ, FL, NC. Most restrictive: NV, ND, NY, MD, VT, ME, IN (EdChoice)
- Missing documents cause 60% of delays in Approval Required states; correction adds 4-8 weeks
- Early legal review saves 2-3 months in Approval Required states (Husch Blackwell)
- ESA/voucher participation triggers additional regulatory layers beyond baseline
