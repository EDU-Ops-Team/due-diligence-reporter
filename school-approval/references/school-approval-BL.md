## DOK4 — SPOVs

### Licensing Is About Predictability, Not Speed
States reward clear, complete, and curriculum-aligned applications — speed follows clarity.

### Curriculum Drives Compliance
Aligning instructional models to state frameworks removes the majority of common approval obstacles. This can mean toning down the "2 hour school day" to more of a "2 hour individualized education program augmented by ed tech apps, followed by 6 hours of life skills workshops and enrichments."

### Most Operators Wait in Line When They Could Walk Through Two Doors at Once
In Notification and Minimal states (TX, FL, AZ, CA, NC — the majority of Alpha School target markets), there is no state education approval process to manage. Operators waste 3-6 weeks preparing registration packages for states that don't require them, while under-preparing for the building CO, which is the actual bottleneck. The discipline isn't caution about concurrency — it's classifying each state's archetype in the first 48 hours after lease signature and calibrating effort accordingly. In Approval Required states (MA, WA, NY, MD), the reverse is true: operators underestimate the approval timeline and discover too late that a school committee or state board controls their opening date.

### Negative Media About Public Schools Can Kill Your Application Before It's Filed
State relationships turn process into partnership, but public information that implies their school system is bad or that administrators are dumb can be highly damaging. Control the flow of media in the market and/or address it head on — put administrators at ease quickly with how much you love teachers, how important education is, and how you support kids in many ways. In Approval Required states where local committees or boards have discretionary authority, political goodwill is not optional — it's a prerequisite.

### The ESA/Voucher Trap: Optional Programs Create Mandatory Complexity
States like TX (TEFA), AZ (ESAs), and FL (FES) have minimal baseline requirements to operate a private school — but participating in state-funded choice programs adds accreditation, testing, financial reporting, and compliance layers that can take months to satisfy. Operators must decide early: open first under minimal requirements, then pursue program eligibility separately? Or delay opening to satisfy program requirements upfront? The wrong sequencing decision can cost a full semester of enrollment revenue.

## DOK3 — Insights

### Insight 1 — State Archetype Classification Determines Everything
**Action:** Within 48 hours of lease signature, classify the state into one of five archetypes: Minimal, Notification, Approval Required, Heavily Regulated, or Windowed/Calendar-Dependent. This single determination drives every subsequent action, timeline, and resource allocation.
**QC:** Archetype logged with supporting statutory citation within 2 business days. Wrong archetype = wrong plan.

### Insight 2 — Early Regulatory Mapping Creates Predictability
**Action:** Identify responsible state department, approval type (registration vs licensure vs none), and review cycle immediately after lease has been signed.
**QC:** Regulatory matrix completed within 5 days of lease signature. Includes: department name, contact, statutory authority, filing requirements, and timeline.
**Impact:** Prevents 3–5 week lag between building approval and licensing start.

### Insight 3 — Curriculum Alignment Determines Depth of Review
**Action:** Map core curriculum standards to state frameworks; identify exemptions for private schools. In Minimal/Notification states, confirm that no curriculum review exists and document the exemption. In Approval Required states, complete curriculum crosswalk before submission.
**QC:** For Approval Required states: curriculum crosswalk completed before submission; alignment documented. For Minimal/Notification states: exemption confirmed with statutory citation.
**Impact:** Cuts iterative feedback cycles by 40–60% in states that review curriculum.

### Insight 4 — Documentation Completeness Predicts First-Pass Approvals
**Action:** In Approval Required and Heavily Regulated states, assemble governance docs, staff credentials, emergency plans, and policy manuals before submission. In Notification states, prepare only what's required (often just an online form).
**QC:** Zero missing elements flagged in pre-submission QA for Approval Required states. For Notification states, confirmation receipt obtained.
**Impact:** Dramatically increases first-pass approval probability in Approval Required states.

### Insight 5 — Local Expertise Prevents Procedural Delays
**Action:** In Approval Required states (especially MA where local school committees have discretionary authority), engage state-specific education attorney or consultant before initial contact with regulator.
**QC:** Legal pre-review completed and annotated checklist logged.
**Impact:** Avoids 2–3 month delay caused by procedural misinterpretation. In Minimal/Notification states, legal pre-review is unnecessary — save the budget.

### Insight 6 — Feedback Loops Shorten Iterations
**Action:** In Approval Required and Windowed states, conduct weekly communication cadence with assigned licensing officer or school committee contact.
**QC:** Outbound contact logged weekly; no gap >7 days without documented follow-up.
**Impact:** Reduces dormant queue time by 30–50%.

## DOK3 — Blueprints

### Fast-Track Registration Checklist

**Domain Context:** Post-lease-signature workflow for achieving operational readiness. Steps vary by state archetype.

**Actions:**

**All States (Day 1–2):**
1. Classify state archetype (Minimal / Notification / Approval Required / Heavily Regulated / Windowed)
2. Confirm whether school will participate in ESA/voucher program (changes requirements)
3. Identify responsible state department and contact information

**Minimal States (TX, AZ, AR, UT, AL):**
4. Confirm statutory curriculum requirements (e.g., TX: reading, spelling, grammar, math, good citizenship)
5. No filing required — focus all effort on building CO (AL non-church schools: register by Oct 10 with AL DOE)
6. If pursuing ESA/voucher: begin accreditation and program application as parallel workstream
7. Goal: Open as soon as CO is issued

**Notification States (FL, CA, NC, DE):**
4. File registration/notice of intent (FL: DOE survey + owner fingerprinting; CA: PSA online; NC: NOI to DNPE 30-60 days before operation; DE: DDOE online application + annual reporting)
5. Obtain fire/sanitation inspections where required
6. Goal: Registration complete before or concurrent with CO. ≤2 weeks.

**Tennessee (Category System):**
4. Determine target category (likely Category II via accrediting agency, or Category V with baccalaureate-degreed teachers)
5. Contact Private.Schools@tn.gov to initiate State Fire Marshal inspection
6. If Category II: begin accrediting agency application concurrent with building work
7. Goal: Approval concurrent with or shortly after CO. 4-12 weeks.

**Approval Required States (MA, WA, NY, MD):**
4. Engage local education attorney (Week 1)
5. Identify approving body and calendar cycle (MA: local school committee; WA: SBE annual cycle; NY: Board of Regents with March 1 deadline; MD: State Board)
6. Conduct legal pre-review and gap analysis (Week 2)
7. Submit complete, pre-audited application package (Week 3-4)
8. Maintain weekly communication cadence with licensing officer (Week 4+)
9. Goal: Achieve approval ≤8 weeks post-submission. Plan for 12-16 weeks worst case.

**Windowed States (NY, WA, MD):**
4. Identify next available approval window immediately — this may be the critical path item
5. If window is >3 months away, evaluate: can you file early? Is provisional status available?
6. Goal: Never discover the calendar constraint after committing to a lease.

**Tools:** State DOE websites, ICC Code Adoption Tracker, county assessor databases, local education attorneys, EdChoice School Starter Checklist for regulatory burden reference.

**Boundaries:** This workflow covers state-level education registration only. Building permits, zoning, and E occupancy are covered by the SIR Jurisdiction Caller and Site Evaluator agents.

**QC / Definition of Done:**
- State archetype classified with statutory citation
- All required filings submitted or confirmed unnecessary
- Approval timeline documented (best-case and worst-case)
- ESA/voucher decision logged with implications
- No open items without documented next steps

## DOK2 — Knowledge Trees

### State Registration Archetype Framework

#### Category Summary
Private school registration requirements vary dramatically by state. The most important analytical tool is classifying each state into one of five archetypes that predict the timeline, effort, and dependencies the operator will face. Most Alpha School target markets fall into the two lightest categories.

#### Archetype 1: MINIMAL — No Registration or Approval Required

| State | What's Required | Key Statute / Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Texas** | No registration, licensing, or TEA approval. Must offer reading, spelling, grammar, math, and "good citizenship." | TEC §25.086; No approval process through TEA |
| **Arizona** | No accreditation, registration, licensing, or approval. File affidavit with county superintendent for students ages 6-16 (notification, not approval). | ARS §15-802 |
| **Arkansas** | No requirements for accreditation, registration, licensing, or approval from the state. May voluntarily accredit through AR Nonpublic School Accrediting Association. | US DOE State Regulation report (AR chapter); no applicable AR statute requires registration |
| **Alabama** | Private schools (non-church) must register annually by Oct 10 with AL DOE and report enrollment/attendance. Church schools are exempt from all requirements except filing with county superintendent. No state approval or licensing. | AL Code §16-28-1, §16-28-5, §16-28-7; AL Code §16-1-11.1 |
| **Utah** | No state approval required. Among least regulated per EdChoice. | EdChoice School Starter Checklist Rankings, April 2025 |

**Timeline impact: 0 weeks post-CO.**

**ESA/Voucher caveat:** TX (TEFA), AZ (ESAs) add accreditation, testing, and reporting requirements IF the school participates in choice programs. These are optional and do not affect the right to operate.

#### Archetype 2: NOTIFICATION — Register/File, No Approval Review

| State | What's Required | Key Statute / Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Florida** | Register with DOE + annual survey. Owner fingerprinting/background check. DOE explicitly has no jurisdiction over private schools. Not licensed, approved, or accredited by the state. | FL Stat. §1002.42 |
| **California** | File Private School Affidavit (PSA) annually with CDE. Online form, immediate confirmation. Filing does not constitute approval or endorsement. | CA EC §33190 |
| **North Carolina** | File Notice of Intent with Division of Non-Public Education. Suggested 30-60 days before operation. Fire/sanitation inspections required. Annual standardized testing required. | NC GS §115C-547 through 562 |
| **Delaware** | Must register with DDOE (online application). Annual enrollment and attendance reporting required (by Sept 30 and July 31). DDOE reviews and opens or denies applications but does not approve, accredit, or monitor curriculum. | 14 Del. C. §2703, §2704; DDOE Nonpublic Schools page |
**Timeline impact: 0-2 weeks. Can run concurrent with CO process.**

**Note on Tennessee:** TN has a complex five-category system for non-public schools (SBE Rule 0520-07-02). Private schools must be approved under one of five categories — either directly by the state (Category I), through an SBE-approved accrediting agency (Category II), through a regional accreditor (Category III), through SBE application for church-related schools (Category IV), or as a Category V school with baccalaureate-degreed teachers. The approval pathway depends on school type. Alpha School sites in TN should target Category II or V. This makes TN closer to Approval Required than Notification, but the accrediting agency pathway (Category II) can run concurrent with building work. State Fire Marshal inspection of the school site is required before approval (TCA §49-6-3001; SBE Rule 0520-07-02). Timeline: 4-12 weeks depending on category and accrediting agency.

#### Archetype 3: APPROVAL REQUIRED — Government Body Must Approve

| State | Approving Body | Key Requirements | Key Statute / Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Massachusetts** | Local school committee (varies by town) | Must demonstrate instruction equals public schools in "thoroughness and efficiency." No state-level appeal if denied. | MA Gen. Laws c.76 §1 |
| **Washington** | State Board of Education | Annual approval. Certificated teachers required. 180 days / 1,000 hours minimum. Health/fire inspections. Curriculum must cover required subjects. | RCW 28A.195.010 |
| **New York** | Board of Regents / NYSED | Not-for-profits need provisional charter. For-profits need commissioner's consent. On-site visit required. | NY Education Law |
| **Maryland** | MD State Board of Education | Certificate of approval required based on facilities, entrance conditions, educational qualifications. Church organizations may be exempt. | MD Education Article §2-206(e) |

**Timeline impact: 4-16+ weeks.** MA is particularly unpredictable — each town operates independently.

#### Archetype 4: HEAVILY REGULATED — Multiple Gatekeeping Requirements

| State | Key Burden | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Nevada** | Traffic studies and architectural renderings required even for <20 students. Most regulated nationally. | EdChoice Rankings 2025 |
| **North Dakota** | Among most heavily regulated per EdChoice. State approval and teacher certification requirements. | EdChoice Rankings 2025; ND Century Code Title 15.1 |
| **Michigan** | Non-church-sponsored schools must show $50,000 cash reserves before opening. | EdChoice Rankings 2025; MI Revised School Code |
| **Indiana** | Among most regulated. Teacher certification requirements. | EdChoice Rankings 2025 |

**Timeline impact: 8-24+ weeks. May require significant upfront capital or professional submissions.**

#### Archetype 5: WINDOWED / CALENDAR-DEPENDENT

| State | Calendar Constraint | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **New York** | Board of Regents meets on fixed schedule. March 1 submission deadline for June approval. | NYSED PSR process |
| **Washington** | Annual SBE approval cycle. Applications reviewed on Board's schedule. | WA SBE; RCW 28A.195.010 |
| **Maryland** | Fixed approval cycles. May block mid-year openings. | MD Education Article §2-206(e); Internal operational data |

**Timeline impact: 0-6+ months of calendar delay on top of review time.**

#### Puerto Rico — LICENSING REQUIRED (Special Classification)

PR requires compulsory licensing for all private K-12 institutions (3 L.P.R.A. §148l). Requirements include: teacher certification from PR Secretary of Education, facilities compatible with school objectives, an education plan, and an economic viability study. Licensing authority sits with the Board of Postsecondary Institutions under the PR Department of State (Law 212 of 2018). Accreditation is optional. **Timeline impact: 8-16+ weeks, highly dependent on staff responsiveness and inspection scheduling. Adaptive — local engagement and pre-briefing meetings are critical.**

### Legal & Regulatory Frameworks

#### Category Summary
Private school registration is governed independently by each state, typically under its Department of Education. There is no federal licensing mechanism. Early comprehension of statutory language, procedural flow, and dependencies with E occupancy shortens total FTO.

#### Sources

##### ED.gov — State Regulation of Private and Home Schools
**DOK1 Facts:**
- Each state independently regulates private school operation; no federal license
- 34 states require some form of registration
- 25 states require some form of approval
- 38 states require some form of licensing
- Proof of facility compliance (CO) is universally required regardless of state archetype
- State-specific intelligence is critical — no two states regulate identically

**Source:** U.S. Department of Education, Office of Non-Public Education

##### EdChoice — School Starter Checklist Rankings (2025)
**DOK1 Facts:**
- Least restrictive states: Delaware, Utah, Arkansas, Arizona, Florida, North Carolina
- Most restrictive states: Nevada, North Dakota, New York, Maryland, Vermont, Maine, Indiana, Alaska, Wyoming
- Eight regulatory categories evaluated: accreditation, nonprofit requirements, school day/year, curriculum, record keeping, testing, teacher certification, health/safety
- 35 states make accreditation optional
- ESA/voucher program participation triggers additional regulatory layers in many states

**Source:** EdChoice, School Starter Checklist Rankings, April 2025

##### Husch Blackwell — Private School Legal Requirements by State
**DOK1 Facts:**
- States vary in whether curriculum review precedes or follows building approval
- Sequential vs concurrent processes cause up to 3–6 month differences in Approval Required states
- Early legal consultation cuts 2–3 months off approval cycles in Approval Required states

**Source:** Husch Blackwell Education Practice

### Academic Compliance

#### Category Summary
Curriculum alignment and instructional integrity form the backbone of approval in states that review it. Most Alpha School target states (TX, FL, AZ, NC) do NOT review curriculum.

#### Sources

##### State DOE Guidelines (various)
**DOK1 Facts:**
- ~70% of states require some curriculum description; ~30% require full documentation
- In Minimal/Notification states, curriculum requirements are limited to covering core subjects — no review process exists
- In Approval Required states, approval speed depends on alignment to state frameworks
- Curriculum mapping reduces regulator questions and review loops in Approval Required states only

**Source:** Various state DOE websites

### Administrative Readiness

#### Category Summary
Operational documentation requirements vary dramatically by archetype. In Minimal states, none is required beyond basic instruction. In Approval Required states, completeness directly predicts approval speed.

#### Sources

##### Sample State Application Packages (MA DOE, WA SBE, NY NYSED)
**DOK1 Facts:**
- Missing or misordered documents cause 60% of application delays in Approval Required states
- Average correction cycle adds 4–8 weeks
- MA requires "thoroughness and efficiency" equivalent to local public schools — each committee interprets this differently
- WA requires certificated teachers or supervision by certificated teacher
- NY requires on-site visit before registration

**Source:** MA DOE Advisory on Approval, WA SBE Private School FAQs, NYSED
