Owner

Greg Foote
Purpose
Decision-Ready Fact Compression The report serves as a decision-support artifact, not a recommendation engine. Its core purpose is to quickly provide leadership with auditable facts to answer three main questions: "Can we do this, what will it take, and how long will it take?".
DOK 4 - SPOV
Focus on Gating Realities and Scenarios Site viability is determined by "gating realities," such as zoning, occupancy, and school approval, rather than just demographics. The report must define the absolute minimum work required to open (MinWork) and the strategic upside case for maximum student capacity (MaxCapacity).
Reflecting Alpha's Operating Reality The final report must reflect Alpha's operating reality, ensuring leadership knows if a site can open quickly, scale credibly, and avoid hidden construction or permitting traps. The minimum work required to reach E-occupancy compliance and open (MinWork) matters most. 

Beliefs
Zoning, occupancy, and school approval are the right lenses because they define whether Alpha can legally operate, what use is permitted, and what approvals or upgrades are required before opening. If one of these lenses fails, the site is not practically viable no matter how strong the market is.
We evaluate MinWork and MaxCapacity because leadership needs to understand both the fastest credible opening path and the full operating upside of the space. MinWork answers what it takes to open safely and compliantly. MaxCapacity answers how far the site can scale if Alpha chooses to invest.

Rules
Treat the SIR as the source of truth for zoning, AHJ interpretation, permitting path, and stated regulatory timelines.
Use the SIR to answer what is allowed, what approvals are required, and what external constraints could block opening.
Treat the Building Inspection as the source of truth for observed physical risk, deferred maintenance, and acquisition-sensitive building issues.
Use the Building Inspection to separate required corrective work from general risk notes and nice-to-have improvements.
Treat the ISP as the source of truth for program fit, room plan, and feasible student capacity.
Use RayCon to price the scenarios defined by the ISP room plan and the compliance requirements identified in the source documents.
MinWork means only the work required to reach E-occupancy compliance and open the building.
MaxCapacity means the highest student count the space can support based on the ISP plan.
If the SIR or Building Inspection cannot be read cleanly, stop trusting automation alone and notify the group for review.
The DDR does not invent facts. It compiles source documents, resolves them into standard fields, and presents the result in a consistent decision-ready format.
